Vision

To provide a platform where students can learn, work and excel in their technical knowledge and have ample access to opportunities where one can acquire skills in the field of applied engineering.

Timeline

2018-19

ERC

European Rover Challenge involved constructing a rover carrying out various tasks on Moon and Mars. The team was successful in achieving the 19th rank in European Rover Challenge 2019 in the world.


2019-20

SUAS

Student Unmanned Aerial Systems - An aerial system is to be built, capable of fully autonomous flight and navigation, payload dropping, obstacle avoidance, etc. The SEDS-VIT team has managed to maneuver UGV(unmanned ground vehicle) autonomously and communicate with the ground station. Final design, flight time calculations and predictions have been done.


2019-20

URC

Team Vyadh, the official team of SEDS-VIT Projects has qualified for the final round of University Rover Challenge (URC) 2020 as one of the top 36 teams in the world.


2019

Pyroboard

To aim was to make a 2D Rubens tube. The SEDS Celestia Projects teams is in the process of making the first prototype


2019

Star Tracker

StarTracker is a telescope mount which will track and display, the object aimed by the telescope on Stellarium in real time. The SEDS-Celestia projects team has completed second prototype.


2019

Quadrone

The SEDS-REC Projects team made a Quadcopter controlled using an RF Transmitter. Installed with a mini FPV Camera with a Video Transmitter and Antenna. Operating Range of 450 m. Very useful for Area Surveillance Applications. The project is completed but the test verification is pending


2018-19

Extremity Rover

The SEDS-REC Projects team designed a Rocker bogie chassis made using PVC Pipes. The rover is controlled via Bluetooth using an Arduino Nano and is capable of treading through rough terrains. The project is completed and test verifications are done.


2019-20

Thrust Vector system

Vector control is employed in commercial spacecrafts and it is not only used for adjusting the heading but is also used for landing (like SpaceX and Blue Origin).


2019

Sounding Rocketry

The aim of the project was to design and launch a two stage Rocket with controlled projectile and a recovery system. The team designed a propeller for a single stage Rocket having sugar candy propellant.
